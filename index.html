Abstract

Large language models applied to financial analysis suffer from miscalibrated confidence, lack of verifiable evidence grounding, and inconsistent multi-dimensional reasoning. FinDebate addresses these limitations through a three-module framework: a domain-specific RAG pipeline using ChromaDB and FinLang embeddings, a team of five parallel specialist agents covering earnings, market, sentiment, valuation, and risk dimensions, and a safety-constrained collaborative debate mechanism that strengthens reports without reversing core investment recommendations.

Evaluated on the Earnings2Insights benchmark across five state-of-the-art LLMs, FinDebate achieves an average improvement of +20.4% over zero-shot baselines, elevating performance from satisfactory (~3.0) to excellent (~3.6) on a four-point institutional quality scale.

Agent Design

Five Specialized Analysts

Each agent is equipped with a two-level prompt structure, a system prompt defining professional identity and an analyst prompt specifying the analytical task and retrieves evidence across four dimensions from the RAG module independently.

Results

Performance Across Models

FinDebate consistently improves report quality across all five base models, with gains ranging from 0.59 to 0.62 points on the four-point institutional quality scale (p < 0.001, paired t-tests).
